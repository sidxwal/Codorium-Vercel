Important Websites

Websites for Practicing Coding Problems :

LeetCode

LeetCode is a popular platform for practicing coding and preparing for technical interviews. It offers a wide range of problems to solve in various programming languages. Users can improve their algorithm and data structure skills by tackling problems categorized by difficulty levels.

HackerRank

HackerRank offers coding challenges and competitions across a wide range of programming topics and languages. It provides a platform for developers to practice coding, participate in contests, and prepare for interviews.

CodeSignal

CodeSignal provides coding challenges and assessments that help users prepare for technical interviews. It offers a variety of problems to practice coding skills in different languages and tracks performance over time.

Exercism

Exercism provides coding exercises and mentorship in various programming languages. It's designed to help developers practice and improve their coding skills with the guidance of experienced mentors.

Coderbyte

Coderbyte provides coding challenges, interview prep resources, and mock interviews. It's a good platform for practicing coding and preparing for technical interviews. Coderbyte offers problems across various difficulty levels, covering topics like algorithms, data structures, and more.

Websites for Useful Free Courses :

Udemy

Udemy offers a wide range of courses on various subjects, including technology, business, and personal development. With both free and paid options, learners can find courses taught by industry experts and enhance their skills at their own pace.

Coursera

Coursera partners with top universities and organizations to offer free courses in a wide range of subjects, including computer science, data science, and personal development. Courses are available in multiple languages and often include video lectures, assignments, and peer-reviewed projects.

freeCodeCamp

freeCodeCamp offers an extensive collection of free coding lessons and projects. It covers various technologies including HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and more advanced topics like machine learning.

Harvard Online Learning

Harvard Online Learning offers free courses from Harvard University, covering topics like computer science, humanities, social sciences, and business. These courses include video lectures, assignments, and readings from Harvard's actual curriculum.

Codecademy

Codecademy offers free interactive coding courses in languages such as Python, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. It provides a hands-on approach to learning programming, with exercises and projects to practice coding skills.

Essential Tools

Visual Studio Code

A versatile and powerful code editor developed by Microsoft, VS Code supports multiple programming languages and provides a vast array of extensions for additional functionality. Best part is that it is totally free to use.

Git

Git is a distributed version control system that tracks changes in source code during software development. It enables multiple developers to work on a project simultaneously, supports branching and merging, and helps in managing project history and collaboration.

Vercel

Vercel provides a free tier with unlimited deployments, automatic HTTPS, custom domains, and seamless integration with Git. It's optimized for front-end frameworks like Next.js, but also supports other static site generators and front-end tools.

NodeJS

Node.js is an open-source JavaScript runtime built on Chrome's V8 engine. It allows developers to execute JavaScript code outside of a browser, enabling server-side scripting and the development of scalable network applications.

LinkedIn

LinkedIn is a professional networking platform that allows users to connect with colleagues, industry experts, and potential employers. It serves as a hub for job searching, career development, and professional networking, providing tools for creating resumes, sharing achievements, and staying updated with industry news. How to Improve your Profile

Make a Better LinkedIn Profile

  1. Profile Picture : Use a clear, professional headshot with a neutral background. Smile and dress in business attire.
  2. Headline : Write a concise headline that includes your job title, industry, and key skills.
  3. Summary : Craft a brief summary (3-4 sentences) highlighting your professional background, skills, and career goals. Mention key achievements and what you are passionate about.
  4. Experience : List your work experience with a focus on accomplishments. Use bullet points to describe key responsibilities and achievements for each role.
  5. Skills & Endorsements : Add relevant skills and seek endorsements from colleagues. Highlight your top 5-10 skills.
  6. Recommendations : Request recommendations from former colleagues, managers, and clients. Provide specific examples of your work for them to reference.
  7. Networking : Connect with industry professionals, join relevant groups, and participate in discussions. Personalize connection requests with a brief message.
  8. Engagement : Regularly share industry-related articles, updates, and insights. Comment on and like posts to increase visibility.

Make a Unique Resume

  1. Format and Design : Choose a clean, professional design. Use a readable font, consistent formatting, and clear section headings.
  2. Contact Information : Include your full name, phone number, email address, LinkedIn profile, and, if relevant, a personal website or portfolio.
  3. Objective or Summary : Write a brief statement (2-3 sentences) that highlights your career goals and what you offer to potential employers.
  4. Experience : Detail your work experience in reverse chronological order. Focus on achievements and use action verbs.
  5. Skills : List relevant technical and soft skills. Tailor the skills section to the job you are applying for.
  6. Recommendations : Request recommendations from former colleagues, managers, and clients. Provide specific examples of your work for them to reference.
  7. Education : Include your academic background, degrees obtained, institutions attended, and notable achievements or honors.
  8. Certifications and Courses : Highlight relevant certifications and courses completed. Include the name of the certification, issuing organization, and date obtained.
  9. Projects and Publications : Include significant projects and publications that demonstrate your expertise. Provide a brief description and outcomes.
  10. Custom Sections : Add sections like “Volunteer Experience” or “Languages” if relevant. Tailor these sections to showcase additional strengths.

Interview Preparation Resources

  • Common Questions : Prepare answers for common interview questions (e.g., "Tell me about yourself", "Why do you want this job?"). Practice concise and confident responses.
  • Behavioural Questions : Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to answer behavioral questions (e.g., "Describe a time you faced a challenge at work").
  • Industry-Specific Questions : Research and prepare for questions specific to your industry. Highlight your expertise and knowledge.
  • Mock Interviews : Practice mock interviews with friends, family, or mentors. Record and review your performance to improve.
  • Dress Code : Choose professional attire appropriate for the company's culture. Opt for business formal unless otherwise indicated.
  • Follow-Up : Send a thank-you email within 24 hours of the interview. Express gratitude, reiterate your interest, and mention a key point from the conversation.

Networking Tips

  • Events and Conferences : Attend industry events and conferences to meet professionals. Participate in sessions and network during breaks.
  • Online Networking : Utilize LinkedIn, Twitter, and other platforms to connect with professionals. Engage with content and join industry-specific groups.
  • Networking Etiquette : Be polite and professional in your interactions. Introduce yourself clearly and follow up after initial meetings.
  • Informational Interviews : Request informational interviews to learn about different career paths. Prepare questions and be respectful of the person's time.
  • Professional Associations : Join associations related to your field. Participate in events, workshops, and networking opportunities.
  • Volunteering : Volunteer for events or organizations to meet people in your industry. Highlight your contributions and skills.
